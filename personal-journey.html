Discovering Noise Music

A Personal Journey into Sonic Chaos and Beauty

  2. Personal Journey

When I first stumbled upon John Cage, Luigi Russolo, and noise music, I’ll admit, I was taken aback. As someone who had always gravitated toward melodies and harmonies, the concept of music without discernible structure or rhythm felt foreign—almost unsettling. However, curiosity got the best of me, and what began as a hesitant exploration quickly transformed into a deeply enriching personal experience.

The First Encounter

My initiation into this music genre happened on a rainy afternoon when I found myself scrolling through a music streaming platform. I was searching for something different, something that would shake me out of my usual listening habits. I stumbled across an album by the avant-garde group Merzbow. The title alone—“A Taste of Feral” — piqued my interest. As I pressed play, a torrent of sounds washed over me: layers of feedback, distorted rhythms, and an unyielding barrage of chaotic textures. It was overwhelming.

At first, I felt a sense of confusion. My brain was trying to categorize these sounds into something recognizable. However, as I let go of that impulse, I started to feel something shift within me. I closed my eyes and allowed the sound to envelop me, transforming my living room into a sonic landscape.

Finding Beauty in Dissonance

With each subsequent listen, I began to appreciate the intricacies of the abrasive music genre. It’s easy to dismiss it as mere chaos, but there’s an underlying beauty that reveals itself upon deeper listening. I discovered that this kind of music often communicates emotions that words and traditional melodies sometimes fail to capture. The raw intensity of these sounds resonated with feelings of anxiety, frustration, and even joy—emotions that felt so genuine and unfiltered.

One memorable track I encountered was by the artist Sunn O))). Their droning guitars and minimalist compositions felt meditative, pulling me into a trance-like state. I realized that it could be a form of catharsis, a way to release pent-up emotions and embrace vulnerability.

A New Perspective on Sound

As my journey continued, I began attending live performances. The energy of a noise concert is unlike any other. It’s immersive and visceral, where the audience collectively experiences the sound. I found myself surrounded by others who shared my curiosity, embracing the unpredictable nature of the performance. The physicality of the sound—how it vibrated through my body—was electrifying.

These experiences opened my ears to the world around me. I began to notice the beauty in everyday sounds: the hum of the refrigerator, the rustling of leaves, the distant sound of traffic. Noise music taught me to appreciate these often-overlooked auditory textures, transforming mundane moments into rich experiences.

Embracing the Unconventional

This obscure music genre has encouraged me to break free from conventional listening habits. I’ve learned to approach music with an open mind, welcoming dissonance and experimentation. This genre challenges me to rethink my relationship with sound, inviting me to embrace uncertainty and chaos.

In a world that often seeks order and predictability, this kind of music has become a refuge—a reminder that beauty can be found in disarray. It has taught me that music doesn’t always have to fit neatly into a box. Sometimes, it’s the unconventional sounds that resonate the most.

Conclusion

My personal experience with noise music has been transformative. What began as a daunting exploration has turned into a profound journey of self-discovery and sonic appreciation. If you’ve ever been hesitant to dive into the world of noise, I encourage you to take the plunge. Embrace the chaos, and you just might find a hidden beauty waiting to be uncovered.
